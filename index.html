Welcome to
Bazadde Care

We provided vocational training to parents, aiming to help them earn a self-sufficient income within two years. The trial was successful, so we expanded the program to more parents with little or no education. With your donations, we'll continue training and equipping parents to live self-sufficiently and care for their families, and also provide basic healthcare to some.

"Bazadde" is a Luganda word that means "one who cares" or "a parent". Bazadde Care is dedicated caring for parents, in other words, Parent Care!

What We Do

Every year we assess some parents and select those who we qualify for our programme. We then EDUCATE the parents on good life principles like biblical lifestyle such as, Jesus is the only Savior, work is from God and those who don't work should not eat. We also teach them how to run a business and the importance of budgeting. We encourage them to save, instead of spending all the money they make. We encourage them to hope in God and have a vision for their lives so that they can starting seeing themselves as successful and happy.

We also EQUIP the parents with vocational skills which they will use to make money. Some are trained in hair dressing, cooking, fixing cars and others. Because of the high unemployment rates in Uganda, we prefer training them to have their own businesses instead of waiting for jobs to come up. After graduation we equip some of the parents with the tools they need to start working and for some of the parents we help them look for jobs.

We believe that GOOD HEALTH is vital in the pursuit of earning an increased income and improving wellbeing, so during this period we check the health status of the parents and their children. We take them to medical centers where they are treated if they have common illnesses. We also teach them good hygiene and encourage them to go for regular checkups.

OUR IMPACT

Some of the parents whose standard of living has been changed include Kalule, who is one of the first parents to join our programme. His life has been transformed from a public toilet attendant in Katanga slum to a barber.
